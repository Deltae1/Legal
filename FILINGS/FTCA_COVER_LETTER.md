Dear General Counsel:

Pursuant to the Federal Tort Claims Act (FTCA), 28 U.S.C. § 2671 et seq., and 28 C.F.R. Part 14, I hereby submit this administrative claim against the U.S. Agency for Global Media (USAGM), including its Voice of America (VOA) division, for damages arising from tortious conduct by USAGM personnel acting within the scope of their federal employment.

**I. CLAIMANT**

Elvin Sangano, U.S. Citizen, formerly employed as Technical Director at VOA/USAGM, Washington DC.

**II. BASIS OF CLAIM**

During my employment at VOA/USAGM, I was subjected to a sustained campaign of workplace harassment, interference with my professional duties, and coordinated psychological pressure by USAGM personnel. Specifically:

1. I experienced deliberate sabotage of a major technical project (ROTV automation upgrade) under my supervision, causing professional harm and extreme stress.

2. I was recruited by the USAGM Security Office, verbally designated as a Confidential Informant (CI), and encouraged to disclose internal workplace information — without written documentation, without legal advisement, and without the protections ordinarily afforded to persons in that capacity.

3. Following my CI designation, I was subjected to a coordinated pressure campaign by USAGM personnel to pursue federal contract bids beyond my individual capacity — conduct I believe was designed to induce procurement fraud and create criminal liability to compel my cooperation with undisclosed interests.

4. I refused to submit any proposal beyond my legitimate qualifications or to engage in any fraudulent activity.

5. The harassment and pressure escalated following my refusal, creating working conditions so intolerable that I was compelled to resign from my position in June 2024.

6. In the same week as my resignation, I was hospitalized at George Washington University Hospital due to the severe psychological harm caused by the foregoing conduct.

7. My request for reinstatement following my hospitalization was denied, despite the fact that a similarly situated white colleague had previously been permitted to resign and return under less extenuating circumstances.

The foregoing conduct by USAGM personnel constitutes, among other torts: intentional infliction of emotional distress, negligent supervision, abuse of process, and deprivation of rights under color of federal authority.

**III. DAMAGES**

As a direct and proximate result of the foregoing conduct, I have suffered:

- Lost wages from June 2024 to present: approximately $366,000
- Medical expenses (hospitalization and ongoing therapy): approximately $25,000
- Future lost earning capacity due to career disruption and reputational harm: approximately $200,000
- Severe emotional distress, psychological trauma, and pain and suffering: approximately $1,773,000

**Total damages claimed: $2,500,000**
