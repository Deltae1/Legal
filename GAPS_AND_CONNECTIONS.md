## HOW TO USE THIS FILE

This file does three things:
1. **Tracks gaps** — what we don't know yet that matters
2. **Tracks connections** — links between people, events, tactics that reveal the architecture
3. **Generates questions** — specific things to ask or verify that could be revealing

It is organized by case layer:
- **LAYER 1** — Employment case (WETA + USAGM) — attorney-facing
- **LAYER 2** — DC operation (ongoing targeting) — FBI/documentation
- **LAYER 3** — Rwanda/financial crime (broader conspiracy) — FinCEN/national security

Gaps are rated by priority:
- 🔴 **CRITICAL** — changes the legal theory or fills a major hole
- 🟠 **HIGH** — significantly strengthens the case
- 🟡 **MEDIUM** — adds texture and corroboration
- 🟢 **LOW** — useful context, not urgent

Connections are rated by confidence:
- ✅ CONFIRMED — directly evidenced
- 🔶 PLAUSIBLE — strongly suggested by pattern
- ❓ SPECULATIVE — possible but not yet supported

### TACTIC 1 — Authority Signaling
**What it is:** Deployment of authority figures (law enforcement,
military, government vehicles) at moments of high stress or legal
activity to create impression of official sanction and surveillance.
**Instances:** Motorcade at attorney Tim meeting (2024); motorcade
April 17 2026; federal vehicles outside Jenkins Row; Capitol Police
motorcade; man with badge at Rasa; National Guard at The Roost;
presidential helicopter; ambulances with lights; fire trucks.
**Purpose:** "We know what you're doing. We have authority. Don't fight."

### TACTIC 2 — Placed Individuals
**What it is:** Deploying individuals into Elvin's environment at
regular locations — gym, grocery stores, coffee shops, transit.
**Instances:** Gym agents; Whole Foods/Safeway individuals; Asian women
recurring pattern; Gigi (social engineering); baby strollers (civilian
cover); Eli (tenant); Jackson (social network).
**Purpose:** Create ambient awareness of surveillance; test responses;
extract intelligence; manufacture social pressure.

### TACTIC 3 — Ambient Signaling
**What it is:** Environmental signals embedded in routine communications
or infrastructure — not overt enough to report, but consistent enough
to be felt.
**Instances:** Ashley water billing emails (May 17 deadline); "limited
stop" transit messages; metro noise level; license plate patterns;
grey vehicle color; construction outside
 building.
**Purpose:** Keep target aware of surveillance without providing
actionable evidence; maintain psychological pressure.

### TACTIC 4 — Displacement Pressure
**What it is:** Coordinated effort to make target uncomfortable enough
to leave a geographic area, specifically DC.
**Instances:** Noise complaints (Eli/Ashley); building alarms; construction;
coordinated comments at The Roost ("shouldn't be here"); isolation from
social support; social media pressure; legal threats (Eli).
**Purpose:** Remove target from legal jurisdiction, attorney access,
and community witness base.

### TACTIC 5 — Social Engineering / Honeytrap
**What it is:** Deploying compromised social contacts to extract
intelligence, drop location, steer behavior.
**Instances:** Gigi (confirmed); Jackson Mvuganyi (confirmed); Eli
(building placement); potentially Aron (luggage).
**Purpose:** Digital surveillance extraction, behavior steering,
micro-compliance testing.

### TACTIC 6 — Four-Element Capture Framework
**Elements:** Focus Capture → Authority → Tribe/Belonging → Emotional
**Instances:** Full April 17 deployment (all five actors, all elements);
USAGM CI designation (Authority); Alain Rwanda call (Tribe); Linda
grandchildren comment (Emotional); motorcade (Authority).
**Purpose:** Systematic psychological capture leading to compliance.

### TACTIC 7 — Micro-Compliance Testing
**What it is:** Small requests or situations designed to test whether
target will comply with minor elements of the scheme before escalating.
**Instances:** Pacific test deposit; Jackson car title errand; ring
emoji (Iliza); Aron luggage (details pending).
**Purpose:** Once target complies even once, leverage increases.
"Once you accept, you become a slave."

### TACTIC 8 — Legal Threat as Distraction
**What it is:** Using manufactured legal disputes to pull target's
attention and resources away from building the real case.
**Instances:** Eli security deposit threat (April 17, same day as
