## Claim-by-Claim Analysis

### 1. Constructive Discharge ★★★★☆ (Strong)

**What works:**
- Hospitalization immediately following resignation is powerful corroborating evidence
- Two simultaneous hostile environments (WETA + VOA) strengthens "intolerable conditions"
- Out-of-character resignation (no offer in hand) supports involuntariness
- Reasonable accommodation upon return implicitly acknowledges the harm

**Gaps to fill:**
- Need exact dates for both resignations — statute of limitations runs from this date
- Need tighter causation chain: hostile acts → resignation → hospitalization
- The resignation letters matter — what did they say? If they cited stress/conditions = gold. If polite/neutral, you need to explain why

**Action:** Locate resignation letters. If gone, WETA and VOA HR must retain them — FOIA or discovery will get them.

---

### 2. Retaliation ★★★★☆ (Strong, needs tighter causation)

**What works:**
- Clear protected activity: reporting to USAGM Security about internal misconduct
- Clear adverse action: conditions worsened after reporting, then constructive discharge
- Timeline close enough to support causation

**Gaps:**
- Need to clearly frame the "protected activity" — reporting to employer's security office about sabotage likely qualifies under Whistleblower Protection Act (if federal employee) or analogous contractor protections
- CI arrangement is double-edged — they may argue voluntary cooperation, not whistleblowing
- Need exact date of security office contact and what happened in days/weeks after

**Critical question for attorney:** Federal employee or contractor at USAGM/VOA?
- Federal employee → Whistleblower Protection Act (WPA)
- Contractor → different, potentially weaker protections

**Action:** Document the security office meeting in full — date, who was present, exact words, what CI arrangement was described.

### 3. Race Discrimination — Disparate Treatment ★★★☆☆ (Viable, needs evidence)

**What works:**
- Legal framework is correct: white comparator reinstated, Black employee denied = Title VII disparate treatment
- Straightforward claim if comparator is documented

**Currently weakest claim because:**
- White colleague is unnamed
- "Similar circumstances" needs to be precise — courts scrutinize comparators carefully
- Need: same supervisor, similar role, similar timeline, different outcome

**What you need:**
- Name or identifying details of white colleague
- Approximate dates of their resignation and reinstatement
- Who made the reinstatement decision (same decision-maker as yours?)
- Witnesses who observed both situations

**Action:** This is worth significant effort. One solid named comparator anchors the entire race claim. Think — who was it? Do former colleagues know?

### 4. Hostile Work Environment ★★★☆☆ (Best used as supporting context)

**What works:**
- Project sabotage is concrete and documentable
- Cross-employer coordination (WETA + VOA simultaneously) is notable
- Social isolation corroborable through witnesses

**Gaps:**
- HWE claims require hostility to be *because of* a protected characteristic (race)
- Submission doesn't currently connect hostility to race — reads as general workplace dysfunction
- Strongest as context supporting constructive discharge and retaliation, not standalone

**Action:** Identify any race-specific comments, exclusions, or differential treatment. Even one documented incident strengthens this.

---

### 5. ADA / Failure to Accommodate ★★☆☆☆ (Include but don't lead with it)

**What works:**
- Accommodation granted (WFH 4 days/week) = implicit acknowledgment of qualifying condition
- Medical records from hospitalization will support

**Gaps:**
- Need formal diagnosis qualifying under ADA (PTSD, anxiety disorder, depression all qualify)
- Stronger if accommodation was denied at any point or hostile conditions continued despite it

**Action:** Obtain medical records, note diagnosis. Therapist documentation of work-related trauma is relevant.

## URGENT — Statute of Limitations

| Claim | Deadline | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| EEOC charge (Title VII — race) | **300 days** from last discriminatory act | DC jurisdiction. Last act = denial of reinstatement |
| EEOC charge (hostile work environment) | 300 days from last act | Continuing violation doctrine may extend |
| Whistleblower Protection Act | **12 months** from retaliation | If federal employee |
| ADA | 300 days | EEOC charge required first |

**If resignation was June 2024 — you are at or near some deadlines right now.**

**File an EEOC charge immediately, even without an attorney.**
You can file online at eeoc.gov. Filing preserves your rights. You can amend later with attorney help.
Missing the deadline permanently kills the claim. This is the single most urgent action item.

## Why You're Not Getting Attorney Callbacks

Most employment attorneys work on contingency and only take clear-cut cases. Your submission is currently presenting a complex, multi-layered narrative. Recommendations:

1. **Lead with the cleanest claims only** — constructive discharge + race discrimination
2. **Keep the broader surveillance/CI/franchise narrative completely separate** for now — it confuses employment attorneys who don't handle national security cases
3. **Target the right attorneys** — general employment firms won't touch federal agency cases
