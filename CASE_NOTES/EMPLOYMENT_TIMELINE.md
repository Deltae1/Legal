### May 31, 2024 — Termination
- **Official reason:** "Termination of convenience" — cited DOGE cuts
- **Actual context:** Termination followed refusal to cooperate with CI pressure campaign
- **Timing:** Termination came directly after the pressure campaign period
- DOGE cuts used as pretext — cover story for retaliatory termination
- **Evidence:** Termination letter in Elvin's possession

**Legal significance:**
- "Termination of convenience" is a federal contracting mechanism — not a performance termination
- Using it as pretext for retaliation while hiding behind budget cuts is actionable
- Timing (pressure campaign → refusal → termination within days) establishes causation
- W-2s from 2020–2022 + termination letter bookend the full employment period

---

### Post-Termination — Benjamin Johnas Recall
- Benjamin Johnas (VOA Director of Operations, retired) asked Elvin to return
- Elvin returned despite the trauma of the CI pressure campaign and termination
- **Significance:** His return was requested — not voluntary job-seeking. He was wanted back.
- This directly undermines any "performance" or "capability" defense

---

### Post-Return — Deterioration Under Pressure
Upon return, Elvin experienced:
- Continuous pressure consistent with the prior CI campaign
- Trauma responses affecting ability to work
- Wanted to quit but sought help instead — important distinction legally

**Legal significance:**
- Returning at Benjamin's request + continuing to try = demonstrates commitment and good faith
- Seeking help rather than quitting = shows this was not voluntary departure
- The pressure continuing post-return = ongoing hostile work environment, not isolated incident

---

### FMLA Request
- Elvin connected with his therapist
- Therapist helped him request **FMLA (Family and Medical Leave Act)** leave
- FMLA was granted — confirms qualifying medical condition related to work trauma
- **Evidence:** FMLA approval documentation in Elvin's possession

**Legal significance:**
- FMLA approval = employer acknowledged a qualifying serious health condition
- Work-related trauma recognized as disabling = connects workplace conduct to medical harm
- Any adverse action after FMLA request = potential FMLA retaliation claim (separate cause of action)

---

### Reasonable Accommodation Request
- Therapist also helped Elvin request a **reasonable accommodation**
- Accommodation approved — **work from home 4 days per week**
- **Evidence:** Reasonable accommodation approval in Elvin's possession

**Legal significance:**
- Employer approved accommodation = formal acknowledgment of qualifying disability
- Approved accommodation + subsequent termination = ADA retaliation
- The accommodation approval is one of the strongest individual documents in the entire case
- It creates a direct legal bridge: disability acknowledged → accommodation granted → termination → retaliation
