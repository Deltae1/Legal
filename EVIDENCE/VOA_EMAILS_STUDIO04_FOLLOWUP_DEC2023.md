## Legal Analysis

### 1. The Iris — "Incorrectly Adjusted"

Benjamin Jonas confirmed:
> "The iris had been incorrectly adjusted and has been reset and saved."

**The iris doesn't adjust itself.** Someone physically or remotely adjusted the
iris incorrectly between the Somali show (which had no issues) and the Burmese
show. This is another instance of equipment being tampered with between shows.

Combined with Elvin's December 3 finding that the Xpression connection
"seems to be deliberately" disconnected — this is a **second instance of
equipment interference documented within days.**

### 2. "The Tech Did Not Reset the Lighting from Somali to Burmese"

Jonas's initial diagnosis: the tech didn't reset the lighting between shows.

But the iris was "incorrectly adjusted" — not merely forgotten to be reset.
An incorrect adjustment is an active change, not a passive omission.

**This matters:** The difference between "forgot to reset" and "incorrectly
adjusted" is the difference between negligence and deliberate interference.
Jonas's final email confirms it was the latter — incorrect adjustment.
